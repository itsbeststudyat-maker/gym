BMI (Body Mass Index): Measures your body weight in relation to your height. It helps you know if you are underweight, healthy, overweight, or obese. (Example: BMI of 22 is healthy).

BMR (Basal Metabolic Rate): The number of calories your body needs at rest to maintain basic functions like breathing, heartbeat, and body temperature. (Example: If your BMR is 1500, your body burns 1500 calories/day even without exercise).

Daily Calorie Needs: The total calories your body needs per day based on your activity level. This includes your BMR plus calories burned by daily activities and exercise. (Example: If your daily needs are 2200 calories, eating more will lead to weight gain, eating less may help lose weight).

Advice: Based on your BMI, this gives simple suggestions like increasing protein, adding cardio, or maintaining current habits.
